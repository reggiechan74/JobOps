02 Phase I: Arsenal Preparation

Find & Save Jobs Worth Pursuing

Stop scrolling job boards aimlessly. Structured search with complete job descriptions saved for analysis.

Manual Upload or /searchjobs

What It Does

Job hunting without a system means applying to everything and hoping something sticks. That's exhausting and ineffective.

This step gives you a structured way to find and save job postings — either manually uploading them to the Job_Postings/ directory or using automated search to discover opportunities worth pursuing. Complete job descriptions are saved locally so you can analyze them properly before deciding whether to apply.

Two Methods

Manual Upload

Save time on copy-pasting. Drop job descriptions you find into markdown files and keep them organized in one place.

Job_Postings/TechCorp_SeniorDev_2025.md

Automated Search

Skip the manual work entirely. Search once and get complete job descriptions saved automatically.

/searchjobs "senior developer" Toronto --save

Search Command Options

  • +
    Keyword Search

    Find jobs by title, skills, or industry keywords without opening 20 browser tabs.

  • +
    Location Filter

    Focus on cities, regions, or remote positions you actually want—no wasted time on irrelevant listings.

  • +
    Company Target

    Zero in on specific companies with --company=name instead of scrolling endlessly.

  • +
    Auto-Save

    Complete job descriptions saved to your local folder automatically—no more lost tabs or forgotten postings.

File Naming Convention

Job_Postings/
CompanyName_Role_Date.md
SearchResults_Query_Date.md — Auto-saved search results

Usage Example

$ /searchjobs "product manager" "San Francisco" --limit=20 --save
Searching hiring.cafe for matches...
Found 20 positions, scraping full descriptions...
Saved: Job_Postings/SearchResults_ProductManager_2025-12-04.md

Don't Get Rejected for Sounding 'Wrong'

A self-promotional American style seems arrogant in Canada. A modest British tone seems weak in New York. Cultural profiles ensure your resume speaks the local language.

The Problem It Solves

Hiring managers have unconscious biases about what a "good" resume looks like ‚Äî and those biases are cultural. Use an American-style resume in Canada, and you'll seem arrogant. Use a British-style resume in Silicon Valley, and you'll seem unconfident. Either way, you're rejected before your skills are even evaluated.

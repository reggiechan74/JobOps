Provenance Hardening

Comprehensive credibility verification that catches fabricated claims before they reach an interviewer.

The Problem It Solves

In an era where 73% of resumes contain embellishments, interviewers are trained skeptics. They will probe your claims looking for inconsistencies. Provenance hardening ensures every statement on your resume can survive interrogation.

How It Works

During the resume building process, provenance analysis performs a comprehensive audit:

1
Evidence Mapping

Every claim in the draft is traced back to your career inventory with specific file and line references.

2
Risk Assessment

Each claim is categorized by defensibility risk: High, Medium, or Low.

3
Recommendation

Specific guidance on how to strengthen or rephrase risky claims.

4
Hardening

Final resume incorporates all recommendations for maximum credibility.

Risk Categories

HIGH RISK

Immediate Action Required

  • Unbounded metrics without timeframes
  • Unsupported superlatives ("market-leading")
  • Cross-document inconsistencies
  • Claims without evidence in inventory
  • Benchmark claims without defined peer sets
MEDIUM RISK

Should Be Addressed

  • Mechanism-free outcomes (results without "how")
  • Duplicate achievements across roles
  • Tool names without measurable outcomes
  • Vague team size or scope references
  • Implied rather than stated causation
LOW RISK

Monitor Only

  • Standard industry terminology
  • Well-documented achievements
  • Specific, bounded metrics
  • Clear mechanism explanations
  • Consistent timeline across documents

What Gets Flagged

  • !
    Inflation Patterns

    "Saved millions" without specific dollar amounts. "Massive improvement" without percentage. These collapse under questioning.

  • !
    Attribution Issues

    Taking credit for team accomplishments. If you led a team, specify your role. If you contributed, clarify your specific contribution.

  • !
    Timeline Gaps

    Achievements that don't align with employment dates. Claims from before you joined or after you left.

  • !
    Scope Creep

    Responsibilities that exceed what's plausible for the role level. Junior engineers don't typically "architect enterprise systems."

Example Analysis

CLAIM

"Led digital transformation initiative that revolutionized company operations"

ANALYSIS
HIGH RISK: "Revolutionized" is an unsupported superlative
MEDIUM RISK: No metrics on impact or scope
MEDIUM RISK: No mechanism explaining how
HARDENED VERSION

"Led 18-month digital transformation reducing manual processes by 60% (saving 2,400 labor hours/month) by implementing ServiceNow workflows across 4 departments"

The Interview Defense Test

Every hardened claim should pass these questions:

1

"Can you walk me through exactly how you achieved that?"

2

"What was the baseline before your involvement?"

3

"How did you measure that outcome?"

4

"What was your specific role vs. the team's contribution?"

5

"What obstacles did you face and how did you overcome them?"

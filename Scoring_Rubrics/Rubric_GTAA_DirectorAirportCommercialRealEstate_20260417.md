## CRITICAL BARRIERS FOR THIS ROLE

**Aviation-Specific Critical Barriers:**
1. **NAS Ground Lease Compliance**: Candidate must understand federal land constraints; no criminal history affecting Transport Canada security clearance
2. **People Management at Scale**: Role explicitly requires 7+ years; candidate with <5 years at small-team scale only is a high risk for a team of 8–15
3. **Subsidiary Governance**: Director is responsible for two legal entities (ACI/MGI); experience managing separate entities with distinct fiduciary obligations is a significant differentiator
4. **Aviation Tenant Dynamics**: Airline tenants are fundamentally different from standard commercial tenants; absence of any aviation/airline stakeholder experience is a medium risk requiring mitigation plan

**Stretch Opportunities for High Potential:**
- Transport Canada March 2025 PPP policy: No airport Director in Canada has yet mastered this new framework; first-mover advantage for candidates with P3/master developer experience
- LIFT commercial integration: Managing leasing design during active terminal construction requires both CRE and project management sophistication; rare combination

## ASSESSOR NOTES

### Key Strengths to Look For
1. P&L ownership for institutional CRE portfolio at comparable AUM ($300M+) with documented NOI outcomes
2. Complex lease negotiation track record with quantified above-market results
3. Team development history showing capability building through change (not just team maintenance)

### Key Risk Areas to Probe
1. Aviation/airport-specific context: how will candidate ramp on NAS regulations, airline tenant dynamics, aeronautical compatibility planning?
2. Subsidiary governance: experience managing separate legal entities with distinct reporting obligations (IFRS 40, board-level accountability)
3. Scale of team management: largest direct report team and how it compares to an 8–15 person Real Estate team

### Interview Focus Areas
1. **Aviation ramp**: "What is your understanding of how airport ground leases differ from standard commercial leases? What would you need to learn to be immediately effective?"
2. **Portfolio recovery**: "The ACI/MGI portfolio took $50M in write-downs 2022–2024. Walk me through your approach to portfolio recovery and occupancy improvement."
3. **PPP origination**: "Transport Canada's March 2025 policy enables new private investment models at NAS airports for the first time. How would you approach originating a master developer partnership for the Airport Road corridor?"
4. **Team building**: "Describe a time you built a diverse, high-performing team from scratch during a period of organizational transformation."

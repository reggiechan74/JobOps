Job Description

The Opportunity

As Director, Real Estate Investments, you’ll lead the sourcing, evaluation, and execution of strategic acquisitions, dispositions, and development projects across Canada. You'll also play a key role in implementing value-add asset management strategies — contributing to the overall performance and growth of Chartwell’s real estate portfolio.

This is a dynamic and cross-functional role that blends strategic thinking, financial acumen, and relationship management, offering the opportunity to directly shape the future of senior living communities in Canada.

What You’ll Do

Lead Strategic Acquisitions & Developments

Build and manage a pipeline of senior housing investment opportunities through proactive sourcing and industry relationships.
Perform financial modeling, valuations, and underwriting to support business cases.
Lead due diligence processes and negotiate terms with partners, brokers, and consultants.


Drive Strategic Dispositions & Repositioning

Manage the disposition of non-core assets, including internal approvals, broker engagement, and buyer negotiation.
Support property repositioning and expansion strategies to optimize portfolio performance.


Support Asset Management Initiatives

Collaborate with internal teams (Operations, Legal, Development, Finance) to support leasing, compliance, audits, and partner reporting.
Contribute to capital planning and investment committee materials.


Build & Manage External Partnerships

Develop and nurture relationships with property owners, developers, commercial brokers, joint venture partners, and financial institutions.
Represent Chartwell at industry conferences and networking events to enhance our profile in the senior housing sector.


What You’ll Bring

5–8 years of experience in commercial real estate, with exposure to acquisitions, dispositions, development, asset management, financial analysis, valuations, real estate underwriting
Proficiency with Excel, PowerPoint, and financial modeling tools.
Post-secondary education in business, real estate, or finance; MBA or designation (CPA, CFA, CBV) is an asset.
Strong communication and presentation skills — persuasive, analytical, and confident.
Highly organized, detail-oriented, and self-directed with the ability to manage competing priorities.
Demonstrated success in stakeholder engagement and deal negotiation.
Comfortable using AI and digital platforms to support day-to-day responsibilities.
